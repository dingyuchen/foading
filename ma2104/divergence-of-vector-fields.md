# Divergence of a Vector Field

Let $U \subseteq \R^n$ be an open set in $\R^n$

and $\vec F : U \rightarrow \R^n$ be a differentiable vector field,

The **divergence of** $\vec F$ is the (scalar) function $\nabla \cdot \vec F \rightarrow \R$ given by

$$\nabla \cdot \vec F = \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial x_1} + ... + \frac{\partial F_n}{\partial x_n}$$

Thus $\nabla \cdot \vec F$ is a function on $U$ is continuous iff $\vec F$ is continuously differentiable.

## Algebraic Rules for Divergence

1. Sum Rule 
2. Difference Rule 
3. Constant multiple Rule 
4. Product Rule $\nabla \cdot (f \vec F) = f(\nabla \cdot \vec F) + (\nabla f) \cdot \vec F$

## Divergence of Curl

If $\vec F$ is a twice continuously differentiable vector field in $\R^3$, then

$$\nabla \cdot (\nabla \times \vec F) = 0$$

If $\vec F$ is a curl vector field, 

then $\nabla \cdot \vec F = 0$

Since $\vec F = \nabla \times \vec G$ implies $\nabla \cdot \vec F = \nabla \cdot (\nabla \times \vec G) = 0$

## Divergence Theorem

Let $D$ be a compact region in $\R^3$,
whose boundary $\partial D$ is a piecewise smooth surface.

Let $\vec F : D \rightarrow \R^3$ be a continuously differentiable vector field on $D$.
Then

$$\int_D (\nabla \cdot \vec F) \; dV = \oint_{\partial D} \vec F \cdot \vec n \; d \sigma$$

where the boundary $\partial D$ is oriented with the unit normal vector field $\vec n$ pointing outwards.

### Divergence Theorem in $\R^2$

Let $D$ be a compact region in $\R^2$,
whose boundary $\partial D$ is a piecewise smooth curve.

Let $\vec F : D \rightarrow \R^2$ be a continuously differentiable vector field on $D$.
Then

$$\int_R (\nabla \cdot \vec F) \; d(x, y) = \oint_{\partial R} \vec F \cdot \vec n \; ds$$
